How Face Shape Is Determined

Face shape is determined by analyzing the proportions of the face. This includes measuring the width of the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, as well as the length of the face from forehead to chin. The relationship between these dimensions defines the overall shape.

Traditional face shape detection relies on visual assessment and measurements, while modern methods use facial recognition technology. Both approaches aim to identify patterns in facial structure that align with recognized face shape categories. Accurate detection requires attention to symmetry, angles, and overall balance.
